TESMAN.

Yes, you don't seem to have wasted your time on your wedding trip,George.

TESMAN.

that I haven't. But do take off your bonnet, Auntie. Look here! Let me untie the strings--eh?

TESMAN.

[While he does so.]

Well well--this is just as if you were still at home with us.

TESMAN.

[With the bonnet in his hand, looks at it from all sides.]

what a gorgeous bonnet you've been investing in!

MISS TESMAN.

bought it on Hedda's account.

TESMAN.

Hedda's account? Eh?

MISSTESMAN.

so that Hedda needn't be ashamed of me if we happened to go out together.

TESMAN.

[Patting her cheek.]

always think of everything, Aunt Julia.

[the bonnet on a chair beside the table.]

now, look here--suppose we

comfortably on the sofa and have a little chat, till Hedda comes.

[They seat themselves.

places her parasol in the corner of the sofa].
